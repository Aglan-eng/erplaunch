## 5. Methodology

### 5.1 Frame

Baseline the operating model. Two-week sprint with the customer's operators to document what's happening today — processes, integrations, owners, exception handling. We don't propose changes until we agree on the current state.

### 5.2 Build

Cut the new system. Configuration, data migration, and integrations land in parallel work-streams under a single project lead. Two-week increments with weekly demo cadence so the customer sees the system come together rather than waiting for a big-bang reveal.

### 5.3 Land

Go live with confidence. The 30-day hypercare window is included in the price — not an upsell. Day-one support is provided by the same engineers who built the system; no handoff to a B-team on go-live.

### 5.4 Sustain

Bridge to managed services. Where the customer wants it, the Orgarise add-on takes the system into long-term operational support — monthly health checks, quarterly optimisations, and an on-call line for the operator team.

## 6. Roadmap

### 6.1 Foundation (months 1-3)

Core NetSuite modules live, baseline KPIs captured, hypercare wrapped. Customer's operators using the new system for daily work; INTILAQA-pattern configuration packages applied.

### 6.2 Expansion (months 4-9)

Adjacent modules layered in (typically reporting, dashboards, multi-entity consolidation, region-specific tax handling). KPI shifts measured against baseline and reported quarterly.

### 6.3 Optimisation (months 10-18)

Process refinement based on usage data, integration deepening, analytics maturity. Customer is now self-sufficient on the platform; Xelerate role shifts to advisor or transitions to Orgarise managed services.

## 9. Industry Verticals

### 9.1 Poultry and Agritech

**Outcome:** Real-time flock-level cost visibility and feed-conversion-ratio reporting at the farm level.
**Strategic context:** GCC poultry operators consolidating from spreadsheet-based farm management onto integrated ERP, often after a rapid expansion phase that outgrew the legacy stack.
**Approach:** Phase 1 financials + inventory for flock-input costing. Phase 2 production module with batch tracking. Phase 3 analytics overlay for FCR + mortality + yield reporting against industry benchmarks.

### 9.2 Precious Metals

**Outcome:** Lot-level traceability from refinery through wholesale, with hallmarking and customs-compliant export documentation.
**Strategic context:** UAE and KSA precious-metals operators serving regional and export markets where lot traceability is both a regulatory requirement and a buyer expectation.
**Approach:** Inventory module with serialised or lot-tracked items first. Add custom certificate-of-authenticity workflow. Layer cost rollup against London fix prices via integration.

### 9.3 Professional Services

**Outcome:** Project profitability the partners can defend, monthly close in five business days, billable utilisation visible per consultant.
**Strategic context:** Consultancies and agencies across MENA running on spreadsheets and email, billing manually, hemorrhaging margin on under-utilised juniors and project overruns.
**Approach:** Time-tracking + project accounting first. Resource utilisation reporting second. Pricing-model overhaul (T+M vs. fixed-fee vs. retainer) once the data tells the story.

### 9.4 Pharma Manufacturing

**Outcome:** Batch records compliant with regional regulator expectations, lot-level expiry and recall capability, accurate landed-cost reporting on imported APIs.
**Strategic context:** Pharma manufacturers across the GCC dealing with mixed local + import supply chains, regulatory submissions in multiple jurisdictions, and quality-system documentation tied to ERP transactions.
**Approach:** Production module with batch + lot tracking from day one. Layer landed-cost reporting against multi-currency supplier invoices. Integration with quality-management systems and regulatory submission tooling.

### 9.5 Manufacturing

**Outcome:** Cycle-time reduction and BOM accuracy at the workstation level, plus accurate cost-of-goods-manufactured reporting that feeds into pricing decisions.
**Strategic context:** Discrete and light-process manufacturers across MENA on legacy MRP systems that can't keep up with multi-line scheduling or multi-currency component sourcing.
**Approach:** Start with the production module + work-order tracking. Layer planning and demand forecasting once the operators are bought in to the new system. Integrate with shop-floor data collection where the operating maturity supports it.

## 10. Voice Guide

Headlines: sentence case. Active voice. No buzzwords. Replace "leverage" with "use", "synergy" with "fit", "best-in-class" with a specific claim ("the only NetSuite partner in the UAE with three healthcare go-lives in 2025"). Numbers always preferred over adjectives — "30-day hypercare" beats "extended hypercare", "200+ implementations" beats "extensive experience."

Body copy: short paragraphs (3 sentences max). One idea per paragraph. Bulleted lists when more than three items. Avoid em-dashes in customer-facing copy; use commas or periods instead.

Region-aware phrasing: prefer "across MENA" to "globally"; "GCC" + specific country names when relevant; never claim "world-class" when "the regional leader" or "the most experienced NetSuite partner in (country)" is the actual claim.

Forbidden words: "robust", "scalable", "enterprise-grade", "best practice", "world-class", "transformational" (unless we can quote a customer using it about us). Never the phrase "trusted advisor" — trust is earned, not asserted. (The exception: "your trusted Oracle NetSuite partner" in marketing copy is fine because it pairs the assertion with a specific platform and a specific role.)
