# Xelerate Brand Pack

This is the canonical Brand Pack for Xelerate, a worked example of the
12-section contract documented in `docs/firm-templates.md`. The Phase
49.5 seed script (`apps/api/src/db/seeds/data/xelerate-brand-pack.md`
is a copy of this file) ingests it into the Xelerate firm row at
deploy time so the proposal generator emits Xelerate-voice copy on
every Xelerate engagement.

## 5. Methodology

### 5.1 Frame

Baseline the operating model. We document what's happening today —
processes, integrations, owners — before we propose what should
change. This is a 2-week sprint with the customer's operators, not a
solo design exercise.

### 5.2 Build

Cut the new system. Configuration, data migration, and integrations
land in parallel work-streams under a single project lead. We work
in 2-week increments with weekly demo cadence so the customer sees
the system come together rather than waiting for a big-bang reveal.

### 5.3 Land

Go live with confidence. The 30-day hypercare window is included in
the price — not an upsell. Day-one support is provided by the same
engineers who built the system; no handoff to a B-team on go-live.

## 6. Roadmap

### 6.1 Foundation (months 1-3)

Core ERP modules live, baseline KPIs captured, hypercare wrapped.
Customer's operators using the new system for daily work.

### 6.2 Expansion (months 4-9)

Adjacent modules layered in (typically reporting, dashboards,
adjacent process automation). KPI shifts measured against baseline
and reported quarterly.

### 6.3 Optimisation (months 10-18)

Process refinement based on usage data, integration deepening,
analytics maturity. Customer is now self-sufficient on the
platform; Xelerate role shifts to advisor.

## 7. Proposal Structure

### 7.1 Introduction

- Anchor the customer's pain in their own words
- Quote the conversation that got us here
- Set expectations for what the proposal covers

### 7.2 Proposed System

- Module-by-module breakdown of what gets configured
- Configuration vs. customisation split (we minimise the latter)
- Integrations called out explicitly with vendor + protocol

### 7.3 Technical Proposal

- Architecture diagram (we always include one)
- Data migration approach with cleanup commitments
- Security + compliance posture for the customer's regulatory regime

### 7.4 Financial Proposal

- Phased pricing aligned to value delivery, not effort
- Optional add-ons priced separately so the customer can choose
- Payment terms tied to milestones, not calendar dates

## 9. Industry Verticals

### 9.1 Retail and Wholesale Distribution

**Outcome:** Single source of truth for SKU-level margin across channels.
**Strategic context:** Omnichannel operators consolidating a patchwork of POS, marketplace, and ERP systems. Most have the data; nobody trusts it.
**Approach:** Phase 1 GL + AR/AP for trustworthy financial close. Phase 2 inventory + fulfilment for operational truth. Phase 3 analytics layer once the data is clean.

### 9.2 Manufacturing and Light Assembly

**Outcome:** Cycle-time reduction and BOM accuracy at the workstation level.
**Strategic context:** Discrete + process MFG operators on legacy MRP systems that can't keep up with multi-line scheduling.
**Approach:** Start with the production module + work-order tracking. Layer planning and demand forecasting once the operators are bought in to the new system.

### 9.3 Professional Services

**Outcome:** Project profitability you can defend to the partners.
**Strategic context:** Consultancies and agencies running on spreadsheets + email, billing manually, hemorrhaging margin on under-utilised juniors.
**Approach:** Time-tracking + project accounting first. Resource utilisation reporting second. Pricing-model overhaul (T&M vs. fixed-fee) once the data tells the story.

## 10. Voice Guide

Headlines: sentence case. Active voice. No buzzwords. Replace
"leverage" with "use", "synergy" with "fit", and "best-in-class" with a
specific claim ("the only NetSuite partner in the UAE with three
healthcare go-lives in 2025"). Numbers always preferred over
adjectives — "30-day hypercare" beats "extended hypercare", "200+
implementations" beats "extensive experience."

Body copy: short paragraphs (3 sentences max). One idea per
paragraph. Bulleted lists when more than three items. Avoid
em-dashes in customer-facing copy; use commas or periods instead.

Forbidden words: "robust", "scalable", "enterprise-grade", "best
practice", "world-class", "transformational" (unless we can quote a
customer using it about us). Never the phrase "trusted advisor" —
trust is earned, not asserted.

## 11. CTA Options

### 11.1 Lock in your kickoff date this week.

Best for: prospects who've validated budget and signed off on the
proposed scope. Use when the next blocker is calendar coordination,
not a decision.

### 11.2 Book a 30-minute working session with our project lead.

Best for: late-stage evaluation when the prospect needs to test the
team, not just the proposal. Pulls the named project lead into the
conversation.

### 11.3 Walk through the architecture diagram with our solution architect.

Best for: technical-buyer-led evaluations where the proposal landed
with the CFO/COO but the CTO hasn't bought in.
